# Cruxia-Labs

We think intelligence is less about capability than the **governance of belief** — holding a belief, revising it correctly when the evidence changes, and proving the change. Today's models mostly *regenerate* rather than *revise*: they re-assert beliefs they were told to drop. We don't claim to have solved this; we build the parts that are deterministic, and we measure the rest.

We started from the smallest corner that's provable today and shipped it as two pieces of **one program**:

- **[sagrada-linter](https://github.com/Cruxia-Labs/sagrada-linter)** — *see it bite:* catch the AI coding rules you already changed before they break your build.
- **[er1-spec](https://github.com/Cruxia-Labs/er1-spec)** — *see the receipt it emits:* a proof a stranger can recompute offline, in any language, without trusting us.

Everything we publish leads with something you can run and ends on a problem we haven't solved yet. — *Mars Ausili*
